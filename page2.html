Operation Brotherhood Montessori began from Operation Brotherhood International, a group known for its humanitarian work in Asia. The organization first helped refugees and later supported poor communities in the Philippines. In 1966, Ambassador Preciosa Soliven founded the first OB Montessori school in Manila. She introduced the Montessori method after studying it in Italy. The school eventually expanded to grade school, high school, and teacher-training programs. OB Montessori also created outreach programs like the Pagsasarili Mothercraft Literacy Program to help underprivileged families. Today, it continues to promote independence, discipline, and social responsibility in its students.
